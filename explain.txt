NeuroScan AI v2.0 — Advanced Brain Tumor Detection System
==========================================================

NeuroScan AI is a state-of-the-art medical imaging application designed to 
assist radiologists and neurologists in detecting and classifying brain 
tumors from MRI scans with high accuracy, explainability, and uncertainty 
quantification.

1. TECHNOLOGY STACK
-------------------
- Frontend: React 18, Vite, Tailwind CSS, Framer Motion (animations), Lucide Icons.
- Backend: FastAPI (Python 3.10+), SQLAlchemy (Async), SQLite/PostgreSQL.
- AI/ML/DL: PyTorch, Torchvision, OpenCV, Scikit-learn.
- Deployment: Uvicorn (ASGI server), GZip compression, JWT authentication.

2. KEY FEATURES
---------------
- AI Tumor Detection: Binary classification (Tumor / No Tumor).
- Tumor Classification: Multi-class (Glioma, Meningioma, Pituitary Adenoma).
- Heatmap Explainability: Grad-CAM++, Score-CAM, and EigenCAM overlays.
- Bayesian Uncertainty: MC Dropout quantification (std dev) and Shannon Entropy.
- Test-Time Augmentation (TTA): 3-view averaged inference for reliability.
- Clinical Reports: Automated PDF generation with clinical staging & WHO grades.
- Scan History: Paginated database of past scans with doctor feedback system.
- Analytics Dashboard: Personal and platform-wide statistics with charts.
- Admin Panel: User management, role promotion/deletion, and platform stats.
- Professional UI: Dark-mode landing page, mobile-responsive dashboard.

3. AI / MACHINE LEARNING ARCHITECTURE
-------------------------------------
- Backbone Models: 
    - EfficientNet-B4: Primary high-accuracy model (19.3M params).
    - ResNet101: Fallback/Ensemble model (44.5M params).
- Advanced Preprocessing:
    - CLAHE: Contrast enhancement in LAB space (highlights tumor edges).
    - Skull Strip Simulation: Removes non-brain tissue to reduce bone noise.
    - Gaussian Denoising: Filters Rician noise common in MRI.
- Inference Pipeline (v2):
    - TTA: 3 augmented views (original, flip, brightness) averaged.
    - MC Dropout: 10 stochastic forward passes to measure "model doubt".
    - Temperature Scaling (T=1.3): Probability calibration to reduce overconfidence.
- Explainability (XAI):
    - Grad-CAM++: Gradient-weighted activation mapping.
    - Score-CAM: Perturbation-based, gradient-free mapping (most faithful).
    - EigenCAM: PCA-based mapping for ultra-fast localisation.

4. TRAINING PIPELINE (train.py)
-------------------------------
- Loss Functions:
    - Focal Loss: Focuses on hard/misclassified examples (imbalance handling).
    - Label Smoothing: Prevents overconfidence during training.
- Augmentations: MixUp, CutMix, RandomPerspective, and GaussianBlur.
- Scheduler: OneCycleLR (Warm-up + Cosine Annealing).
- Optimization: AdamW with Differential Learning Rates (Backbone 10x slower).
- Sampling: Class-weighted WeightedRandomSampler for imbalanced datasets.

5. CLINICAL RISK ANALYSIS
-------------------------
- Graded Severity: Critical (Red) -> High -> Moderate -> Low -> None (Green).
- WHO Staging: Integrated clinical notes for WHO Grade I-IV lesions.
- Confidence Weighting: Automatically upgrades risk if model is uncertain.
- Urgency Levels: Routine, Semi-Urgent, Urgent, and Immediate categories.

----------------
1. Run `.\start_project.ps1` to install dependencies and start servers.
2. Visit http://localhost:3000 to see the Landing Page.
3. Register/Login to access the Dashboard.
4. Upload an MRI (JPEG/PNG) to receive AI analysis + Heatmaps.

9. TECHNICAL QUESTIONS & ANSWERS (Q&A)
--------------------------------------

Q1: Why was EfficientNet-B4 chosen over ResNet101 or VGG19?
A1: EfficientNet uses "Compound Scaling" which scales depth, width, and resolution 
    simultaneously. EfficientNet-B4 achieves higher ImageNet accuracy (84.0%) 
    than ResNet101 (77.4%) while having 2.3x fewer parameters. This leads to 
    faster inference, better generalisation, and a reduced memory footprint.

Q2: What is the benefit of using CLAHE preprocessing on MRI scans?
A2: Contrast-Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalisation (CLAHE) enhances local 
    contrast within an image. In MRI, tumor regions often exhibit subtle intensity 
    gradients. CLAHE boosts these without over-amplifying the white-noise common 
    in T1/T2 marrow — improving the model's ability to detect tumor boundaries.

Q3: Why use both Grad-CAM++ and Score-CAM?
A3: Grad-CAM++ is gradient-based and fast, but can be noisy or "unfaithful" in 
    deep networks. Score-CAM is gradient-free and uses channel perturbations to 
    score activation maps. It is far more faithful to the actual decision-making 
    process of the CNN, although it is computationally more intensive.

Q4: What is MC Dropout, and how does it quantify uncertainty?
A4: Monte Carlo (MC) Dropout treats Dropout as a Bayesian approximation. By 
    leaving Dropout enabled at inference time (usually it's disabled), we get 
    stochastic results. By running 10 forward passes, we can compute the standard 
    deviation (uncertainty). High std dev means the model has "doubt" about its 
    own classification.

Q5: Why is TTA (Test-Time Augmentation) integrated into the inference pipeline?
A5: TTA averages predictions from different views (original, flip, brightness). 
    If a tumor is only visible from a certain "angle" or lighting, the average 
    is far more stable and accurate than a single-pass prediction.

Q6: How does the system handle class imbalance during training?
A6: We use three methods: (1) WeightedRandomSampler to over-sample minority 
    tumor types, (2) Focal Loss to focus on hard/misclassified samples, and 
    (3) Class-weighted BCE/Cross-Entropy during the backpropagation step.

Q7: What is the purpose of Temperature Scaling (T=1.3)?
A7: Deep neural networks are known to be "overconfident" even when wrong. 
    Temperature Scaling calibers the output logits, softening the probability 
    distribution to more accurately match the model's true empirical accuracy.

Q8: Why is "Shannon Entropy" used as an additional metric?
A8: Entropy measures the "disorder" or flatness of the probability distribution. 
    If probabilities are [0.5, 0.5], entropy is high. If [0.99, 0.01], entropy 
    is low. It's a key indicator of whether the model is "confused" between 
    multiple tumor types.

Q9: What is the significance of "Skull Stripping"?
A9: Skull stripping removes non-brain tissue (scalp, skull, eyes) from the 
    image. These regions contain redundant high-contrast features that can 
    mislead the AI. Removing them focuses the CNN purely on the parenchyma 
    where tumors actually occur.

Q10: Why use OneCycleLR instead of standard StepLR?
A10: OneCycleLR starts with a low LR, warms up to a peak, then decays with a 
     cosine curve. This "super-convergence" helps find wider minima in the loss 
     landscape, leading to better generalisation on unseen MRI data.

Q11: Why is Async FastAPI better than standard Django or Flask for this?
A11: AI models are CPU/GPU-bound operations. Async FastAPI allows the server 
     to handle thousands of concurrent metadata requests (history, analytics) 
     without blocking, while the GPU workers process the image analysis in 
     the background.

Q12: Is the system compatible with multi-GPU setups?
A12: Yes. The training script uses PyTorch's `device = 'cuda'` logic. If 
     `DataParallel` or `DistributedDataParallel` is added (not currently 
     implied in v2.0 but easy to extend), it will scale across all available cards.


10. WORKFLOW QUESTIONS & ANSWERS (Operational Flow)
---------------------------------------------------

Q1: What happens exactly when I click 'Upload MRI'?
A1: (1) The image is sent to the FastAPI server. (2) Preprocessing (CLAHE + Skull 
    Strip) is applied. (3) 3 TTA views are generated. (4) 11 forward passes (1 TTA 
    + 10 MC Dropout) are run. (5) Explainability maps (CAMs) are generated. (6) 
    Result is saved to the SQLite history and returned to the UI.

Q2: How does the Feedback Loop improve the system?
A2: Doctors can 'Confirm' or 'Reject' an AI prediction. This feedback is stored 
    with the `ScanRecord`. This ground-truth data can be used for "Active Learning" 
    in future versions to retrain the model on the edge cases it failed.

Q3: How is the Analytics Dashboard populated?
A3: The backend runs SQL aggregations (Count, Avg, Group By) on the `ScanRecord` 
    table. It calculates the detection rate, average confidence, and weekly 
    activity trends which the frontend renders as animated charts.

Q4: What is the workflow for generating a Clinical PDF?
A4: The frontend sends the structured prediction result to `/predict/report`. 
    The `report_generator.py` uses `fpdf2` to create a paginated PDF with 
    the MRI image, the Grad-CAM heatmap, clinical staging notes, and a 
    physician's sign-off section.

Q5: How does an Admin manage users?
A5: Admins access the `/admin` panel. They can view a searchable table of all 
    registered users, promote regular users to 'Admin' status (allowing them 
    to see platform-wide stats), or delete accounts to clean up data.


11. MAJOR INNOVATIONS (NeuroScan v2.0)
--------------------------------------

1. Bayesian "Model Doubt" Quantification: Unlike standard AI that "guesses," 
   NeuroScan uses MC Dropout to measure its own uncertainty. If it doesn't 
   know, it admits it.

2. Ensemble Feature Fusion: By combining the global features of ResNet101 with 
   the inverted-residual efficiencies of EfficientNet-B4, we achieve a more 
   robust classification that resists over-fitting.

3. Triple-Method Explainability: We don't just show one heatmap. We allow 
   radiologists to toggle between Grad-CAM++, Score-CAM, and EigenCAM to 
   verify tumor localization from multiple mathematical perspectives.

4. Contrast-Preserving Preprocessing: Our CLAHE-based pipeline is specifically 
   tuned for the 12-bit dynamic range of medical DICOM/MRI images, preserving 
   pathological details that standard AI resizing often destroys.
